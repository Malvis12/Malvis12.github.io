Frequently Asked Questions

How is GPA calculated?

GPA is calculated by dividing the total number of quality points earned by the total number of credit Units. Quality points are determined by multiplying the grade point value by the credit Units for each course.

What is considered a good GPA?

At Welspring University, a GPA of 3.0 or higher is considered good. A GPA of 3.5 or higher is considered excellent and may qualify you for academic honors.

Can I improve my GPA?

Yes, you can improve your GPA by earning higher grades in future courses. Some programs also allow you to retake courses to replace lower grades, but policies vary by department.

Are all courses counted in my GPA?

Most credit-bearing courses count toward your GPA. However, courses taken pass/fail, transferred credits, or withdrawn courses typically do not affect your GPA calculation.
